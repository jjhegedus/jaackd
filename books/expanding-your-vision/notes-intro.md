# A Note on the Conversations

This book grew out of a long series of conversations between the Author and an AI assistant.

Not conversations in the casual sense — not brainstorming or fact-checking or asking for help with a sentence. These were working sessions where the Author brought half-formed ideas about what he had learned playing pool, and the AI pushed back, asked hard questions, connected things, and forced precision. What started as attempts to describe what good players do differently slowly became a theory — about perception, movement, commitment, and what it actually means to learn something physical.

The exchanges included at the end of each chapter are a record of that process. They are lightly edited: obvious tangents and duplicate threads have been removed, and image links or navigation artifacts from the original chat export have been cleaned out. Otherwise, they are intact.

The Author's prompts appear under **Author.** The AI responses appear under **AI.**

---

## Why include them?

A few reasons.

First, transparency. The ideas in this book did not arrive fully formed. They were worked out in dialogue, tested against objections, and revised when they broke. Showing that process felt more honest than presenting the conclusions as if they had always been obvious.

Second, the conversations are themselves a demonstration of the book's subject. Learning to see — really see — what is happening in a complex skill requires slowing down, naming things precisely, and being willing to be wrong about what you thought you understood. That is what these exchanges look like in practice.

Third, some readers will find the raw reasoning more useful than the finished prose. The chapters distill the ideas. The conversations show how the Author got there — including the wrong turns.

---

## A note on memory

There is a complication worth naming. These conversations were not only about writing a book. They were also about reconstruction.

The Author played pool seriously in college in the late 1980s and early 1990s. He developed a way of seeing the table — a set of intuitions about geometry, attention, and commitment — that made him genuinely good. Then he stepped away from the game for twenty-seven years.

When he returned to pool at 54, during a difficult period of recovery, he found that the intuitions were still there in some form but that he could no longer fully articulate them. The conversations in this appendix are partly an attempt to do that — to name what he did then, to understand why it worked, and to rebuild it deliberately rather than just waiting for it to return.

The difficulty is that he cannot always be certain where the line falls between what he understood in college and what he is projecting backward from where he stands now. Some of what appears in these pages as the thinking of a twenty-year-old player may be the thinking of a fifty-four-year-old reconstructing him. That uncertainty is honest and is itself part of the story.

---

## How they are organized

The conversations are presented in the order they occurred — chronologically, as they happened. This is deliberate. The thinking in these exchanges was not linear, and reorganizing it by chapter would obscure how ideas arrived, how they connected to things said earlier, and how they sometimes reversed course before settling.

Each exchange carries a note indicating which chapter of the book it most closely informed. That label is approximate. Many conversations touched several chapters at once, and some of what was worked out here extended into territory that will appear in later volumes. Where a conversation did not map clearly to any single chapter, it is labeled General Notes.

The conversations continue across the full arc of this series. What is included here is the record through this first volume. Conversations that apply to later volumes were included here for timeliness. AI capabilities are rapidly changing and these conversations are illustrative of interacting with AI at the moment this book was being written. As AI capabilities change, the nature of the conversations will change. These conversations will be most useful for interacting with AI now.
